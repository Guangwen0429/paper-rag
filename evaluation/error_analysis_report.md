# Paper-RAG Chunk-Level Error Analysis

---

> ## ⚠️ Addendum: Conclusions Revised After Chunk-Level Verification (Day 6)
>
> This report was generated by `scripts/analyze_results.py`, which uses
> **character-level keyword matching** as a proxy for "is the expected
> answer present in the retrieved chunks." Manual chunk-level inspection
> via `scripts/inspect_question.py` on 6 failure cases (Q06, Q07, Q11,
> Q12, Q14, Q15) revealed that **this proxy is systematically unreliable
> for numeric and short-word keywords**.
>
> ### Corrections
>
> **Section 4 (Keyword Presence Audit) — false positives identified**:
>
> - **Q11** marked "all" (7/13/70 present): In reality, `7` appeared as
>   page number "77" and benchmark scores (`70.0`, `70.7`); `13` appeared
>   in benchmark rows for LLaMA 1, not as LLaMA 2 release size. The
>   actual release statement chunk (`#1831` in the full chunk list,
>   containing *"releasing variants of Llama 2 with 7B, 13B, and 70B
>   parameters"*) was **never retrieved** in any of the 4 configs.
> - **Q14** marked "all" (8/12 present): In reality, `8` appeared as
>   citation reference `[8]` and year `2018`, not as Transformer base
>   heads count. The authoritative sentence *"we employ h = 8 parallel
>   attention layers"* (Transformer §3.2.2) was **never retrieved**.
>
> **Section 5 (Failure Classification) — both rows below are wrong**:
>
> - ~~Q11 → Generator failure~~ → **Actual: Retrieval failure** (embedding
>   blind spot; authoritative chunk exists but vector/BM25 cannot surface it)
> - ~~Q14 → Generator failure~~ → **Actual: Retrieval failure** (authoritative
>   chunk semantically diluted by surrounding LaTeX formulas; the "16 heads"
>   answer comes from BERT paper citing Transformer *big* variant, not from
>   intra-document confusion as previously hypothesized)
>
> **Q12 partial-retrieval diagnosis — incomplete**:
>
> - Three keywords (`supervised`, `reward`, `PPO`) all appear in retrieved
>   chunks but **scattered across 3 different chunks**, each in an unrelated
>   semantic context (deployment history / ablation study / abstract).
>   The canonical "Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3" chunk from InstructGPT §3.1
>   was **never retrieved**. Classification: **Retrieval failure**, not
>   "partial retrieval".
>
> ### Corrected failure-mode distribution (hybrid k=5)
>
> All 6 failed questions (Q06, Q07, Q11, Q12, Q14, Q15) are **retrieval
> failures**, not generator failures. See `experiment_log.md` Day 6 for
> the 9 distinct retrieval failure modes identified, each with chunk-level
> evidence.
>
> ### Methodological takeaway
>
> Automated keyword-matching metrics are a useful first pass but cannot
> substitute for chunk-level manual inspection. This report's Section 5
> classification is retained below as a cautionary record of how proxy
> metrics can systematically mislead when the proxy (character match) is
> decoupled from the target (semantic fact presence).
